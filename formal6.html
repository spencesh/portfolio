Research Section

The importance of medical research is immense. Professionals in the medical field have to keep up to date in their research, and this is done through different programs, medical journals, and interaction with knowledgeable colleagues. Even if personally the physician does not participate in research it is important that they can interpret the results and apply it to their practice. There is a huge difference in the way information is communicated among researcher to researcher and how it is transferred from researcher to patient. Looking at researcher to researcher communication, in order for one's findings of any research to be accepted as fact, to become an accepted and practiced part of scientific knowledge, it must first be discussed and reviewed by other researchers. This process is very formal. Scientists have to summarize their research in scientific papers and these papers are then published in professional journals. This is how almost all scientific knowledge is created. Which explains how it is constantly changing, since new research rapidly replaces the old, "medical research, like law, is constantly changing. Current information is crucial to the medical researcher. Periodical literature, therefore, is of great importance and in this area, indexes of journals reign supreme" (Leiter) stated from a trade publication. Researcher to patient communication is more of a chain reaction. The researcher information first has to be accepted as fact as said earlier, then as doctors study to further their knowledge they come across the new information from journals. They put these new facts into their practice after many trials and experiments. Then these further knowledged doctors can share verbally to their patients any new diagnosis or treatments that may impact them. These are two very different ways of communicating research, but in all of the steps, literacies are the backbone for it to be a successful process. I conducted an interview with my teacher assistant for chemistry who does research in the science field. She discussed that research topics in chemistry covers a broad range: from highly theoretical to practical. She explained that while some research seems to inform only chemistry theory, many research groups work on topics with current industrial application in areas such as agriculture, pharmaceuticals, and energy technology, "Research on chemistry education works to further our understanding of how students learn and reason about chemistry topics so that we may improve the ways in which we teach" (Kohn). She went over the facts that research findings are communicated formally through peer-reviewed journals, seminars, and conferences. Also, on a daily basis and among members of a research group, communication will occur through email and discussion. I asked how technology has impacted research today, she responded with, "the internet has improved our ability to communicate with other scientists by placing years of journal publications at our fingertips. Journal and discipline-specific search engines allow us to sift through the vast amount of available knowledge based on criteria such as the authors name or a specific chemical structure" (Kohn).
