Background Information

Medicine is known as the field of applied science related to the "art" of curing by diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of disease and illness. It incorporates a variety of health care practices whose goal is to maintain and restore health by the prevention and treatment of illness for their patients. The education for this disciple varies around the world. Normally it involves an undergraduate degree, graduate degree at a medical school, internship, and residency, before one can actually practice in the field. With medicine always advancing and evolving, it is required to keep up to date in education in the field, which can include classes, medical journals, seminars, conferences, and programs online. Medicine is not an exact science in the way that mathematics and physics are. It does have many general principles that are valid most of the time, but not every patient, diagnosis or treatment is ever the same. With that being said medicine is mostly experimental which takes research to a whole other level. The most accepted diagnosis and treatments still need to be monitored, because its never certain that it will work perfectly for the patient, and that is where research comes into play majorly in the medical field. There is constant research being done for medicine, new treatments, new diagnosis, new pharmaceuticals, new surgery, all need to be researched ahead of time before ever taking place. There are so many unanswered questions concerning the medical field, there will always be research taking place because there will never be a time where all the questions are answered. It is an evolving and experimental field that it is crucial to have research in the discipline. Medicine today is just as much about communication as it is science. The only thing that connects all aspects of the medical field is communication, without it medicine could never evolve into what it is today and what it will be in the future. Not only is communication important from doctor to patient, but behind the scenes it is very vital to have strong communication among doctors. They doctors and health care providers have to communicate their research. This is often done in journals and seminars. This sharing of research is vital for the field, just as much as communication among doctor and patient is, because without the sharing of knowledge between professionals, patients will never get the information that they need shared with them.
